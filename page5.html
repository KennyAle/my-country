Discover Ecuador's Thrilling Adventures

In the country of diversity, Adventure sports are not far behind. Each of the four worlds that make up Ecuador (Pacific Coast, Andes, Amazon and Galapagos) has a way of releasing adrenaline in our body: either by climbing rocky walls, furrowing at high speed the torrential rivers that descend into the jungle , surfing in the waves of the Coast and diving in Galápagos, there is an ideal activity for every adventurer.

All these experiences can be lived almost at the same time, because Ecuador combines thousands of fascinating scenarios in a small space.

Paragliding

Ecuador is a great destination to do this activity because of the diversity of the landscapes that can be observed. Whether on the South Pacific Coast or in the diverse Andean scenarios that combine lakes and mountains, every minute in the air will stay in your memory forever.

Where to do it?

  • Cerro Blanco

    Where previously a quarry worked, today it operates a runway near the city of Guayaquil. The view in that locality is dazzling, because you can admire the city and the Cerro Blanco Ecological Reserve. Difficulty: Low. Best season: From May to December.

  • Pululahua

    This geobotanical reserve of volcanic origin allows paragliders to enjoy amazing aerial landscapes in the middle of the world (zero parallel). Paragliders from all over the country gather here. Difficulty: medium, high. Period: all year.

  • Crucita

    It is an ideal place to admire the landscape of the Pacific Coast, in Manabí. Its climate is generally good all year round; which allows the development of the activity in favorable weather conditions. Difficulty: Medium. Period: All year.

Camping

Ecuador has a great natural beauty, admirable throughout its territory. This feature allows several activities to be developed in it. Especially those that allow overnight, such as camping. This practice is the one favored by lovers of freedom and by those who seek to relax outside the city.

Where to do it?

  • Quilotoa Lagoon

    Located in the province of Cotopaxi and occupying the interior of a volcano crater, is the Quilotoa Lagoon. This natural attraction is characteristic for enjoying turquoise waters that merge with the landscape, simulating a fantasy environment. It is one of the perfect places to camp on the shore; light a fire and admire more of the sunset, the snowy ones like the Cotopaxi and the Illinizas.

  • Ayampe

    Located in the province of Manabí, a small paradise that intertwines the jungle with the sea, it is presented and at the same time it hides from the world … it is Ayampe, a reserved and ideal place to find peace and relaxation. Camping near the sea, sleeping listening to the waves and waking up with the sunlight, are some of the experiences that will make you fall in love with the ecosystem that surrounds this wonderful beach.

  • Yasuní National Park

    The Yasuní National Park, located in the heart of the Ecuadorian Amazon and surrounded by a million hectares of tropical forest, besides containing an infinite variety of fauna, allows camping surrounded by a unique landscape, next to the Lagoon of Jatuncocha.

Trekking

The Andes have offered, since the time of the Incas, beautiful roads that unfold between forests, moors, wetlands and more. Today it is possible to travel these old routes and also to trace new little-explored roads that keep a surprise in each section.

Where to do it?

  • Trekking of the Condor

    Departing from the Antisana Ecological Reserve, you can see various climatic floors, lagoons, páramos, snow-capped mountains and more during the journey. An effort in which each step is worthwhile. Near Quito. Difficulty: high. Recommended time: from June to September.

  • The Inca Road

    Also called Qhapaq Ñan, this section, one of the most popular in Ecuador, goes from the Culebrillas lagoon, located at almost 4 thousand meters above sea level, to the fairly well-preserved Inca ruins of Ingapirca, in the south of the country. Difficulty: medium – high. Period: April-December.

  • Cochasquí – Mojanda

    The route starts at the Cochasquí Archaeological Park, where the ruins of an ancient pre-Columbian astronomical observatory are located, and it advances north until reaching the beautiful Mojanda lagoons. Difficulty: medium.

Rafting

The fast rivers that descend from the Andean glaciers are roads that allow us to see how nature transforms. The activity of rafting in Ecuador is carried out on those who irrigate the Amazon rainforest. Several rivers grant the ideal characteristics to guarantee an unforgettable adventure.

Where to do it?

  • Jatunyacu

    It offers a perfect combination of exciting waters between rapids and breaks, so this river is one of the most famous of all the Amazon to perform this sport. Period: All year. Difficulty: Medium.

  • Quijos

    This river of world transcendence (category IV, ideal for the most experienced) attracts visitors from all over. Enjoy the challenge of tamering these crystal clear waters. Period: All year. Degree of difficulty: High.

  • Paute

    This tour that takes place in the Ecuadorian Austro allows us to admire diverse landscapes of the montane forest and agricultural crops, in one of the safest and quietest walks of this sport in Ecuador. Recommended time: all year. Degree of difficulty: Low – Medium.
